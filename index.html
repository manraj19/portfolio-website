Internship Reflection

Download Reflection

What were your expectations about your internship before you joined?

Since the internship was unpaid, I expected to learn and shadow other employees a lot more than getting any actual tasks assigned such as resolving tickets or managing users. I also expected to gain exposure to the corporate culture.

What was the reality? How was it different from your expectations?

Initially, the reality was similar to what I expected, but later on I was given real responsibilities, such as building a change management system for the company. I learnt a lot about IT and data analytics and how they affect day-to-day operations in a real-world setting.

What lessons were the most important from your internship, and why were they important?

I learnt that regular communication, feedback and mutual respect in a workplace are essential. Communication keeps your teammates updated on your progress or when you are facing difficulties. Regular feedback helps individuals and teams improve their performance. Mutual respect is essential because good ideas can come from anyone. Even as an intern, I felt that my opinions were valued by everyone, which showed me how important an open and supportive culture is.

After your workplace experience, what would you say your value proposition to an employer is? How can you demonstrate this?

I can offer strong technical skills combined with a practical understanding of how technology supports business operations. I am comfortable working with both technical systems and non-technical stakeholders.

How did your internship influence the type of role in which you are interested?

While my main interest is still software engineering and development, this internship made me realise that I also enjoy working with IT systems, infrastructure and hardware. I am now more open to roles that combine software development with practical IT and business support.
